Just to let you know, here you have the good new:
http://www.webupd8.org/2011/05/gnome-shell-is-finally-available-in.html

Note for those who are not up to date with everything that's going on
in Ubuntu: GNOME Shell is not and won't be installed by default in
Ubuntu 11.10 Oneiric Ocelot.
However, Ubuntu 11.10 will use GNOME 3 with Unity instead of GNOME Shell.

It's nice to now for our user, because at now, every desktop: Unity,
KDE (obviously), and GNOME, can handle our program with a decent look
and style.

Maybe some of our users wanted to try it, if so, just write:

sudo apt-get update
sudo apt-get dist-upgrade
sudo apt-get install gnome-shell
